It’s time to rethink the fundamental building block of AI: the MLP. 🧠

For decades, we’ve relied on Multi-Layer Perceptrons. The recipe is always the same: multiply inputs by weights, sum them up, and pass them through a fixed activation function (like ReLU). 

But what if the activation function didn't live on the node? What if it lived on the *edge*?

Enter Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks (KANs). 🚀

Unlike standard MLPs, KANs have no linear weight matrices and no fixed activation functions. Instead, every single connection between nodes is a learnable, dynamic 1D function (parameterized as a B-spline). 

Why does this matter?
✅ Better interpretability (you can actually look at the mathematical curves the network learns).
✅ Mathematical elegance rooted in the Kolmogorov-Arnold representation theorem.
✅ The potential to achieve the same accuracy as an MLP with vastly fewer parameters, especially in scientific and mathematical tasks.
